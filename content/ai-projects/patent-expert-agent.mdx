# Patent Intelligence AI Agent

## Overview

The **Patent Intelligence Agent** automates the complex task of analyzing biotech patent landscapes, assessing freedom-to-operate (FTO), and tracking competitive IP strategies—transforming days of manual legal research into hours of AI-assisted analysis.

### The Problem

Patent analysis in biotech is:
- **Time-intensive**: 20-40 hours per landscape analysis
- **Expertise-dependent**: Requires both scientific and legal knowledge
- **Error-prone**: Easy to miss relevant patents in 100,000+ patent databases
- **Expensive**: Outside counsel charges $500-1,000/hour

**Typical FTO analysis cost**: $50,000-100,000
**Time to complete**: 4-8 weeks

### The Solution

An AI agent that:
- **Searches** global patent databases (USPTO, EPO, WIPO)
- **Analyzes** claims, specifications, and prior art
- **Assesses** freedom-to-operate risks
- **Tracks** competitive filing strategies
- **Identifies** white space opportunities

## Key Capabilities

### 1. Automated Patent Search

Intelligent search across:
- **USPTO** (United States Patent and Trademark Office)
- **EPO** (European Patent Office)
- **WIPO** (World Intellectual Property Organization)
- **National offices** (China, Japan, Korea, etc.)

**Search strategies:**
- Keyword + classification codes (IPC, CPC)
- Chemical structure similarity
- Sequence alignment (for biologics)
- Assignee tracking (monitor competitors)

**Output:** Ranked list of relevant patents with relevance scores.

### 2. Claim Analysis

Extracts and structures patent claims:
- **Independent vs dependent claims**
- **Claim scope** (broad vs narrow)
- **Key limitations** (specific to...?)
- **Dependent claim trees**

**Example:**
```
Independent Claim 1:
A method of treating cancer comprising administering
an antibody that binds PD-1, wherein...

Limitations:
- Specific epitope defined
- Dosing range: 1-10 mg/kg
- Patient population: 2L+ NSCLC
- Combination with chemotherapy required

Risk Assessment: MEDIUM
- Our target differs by 3 amino acids
- Our dosing is 0.5-2 mg/kg (outside range)
- We target 1L patients (non-overlapping)
```

### 3. Freedom-to-Operate (FTO) Assessment

Evaluates FTO risk for new products:
- **Identifies** blocking patents
- **Analyzes** claim coverage
- **Assesses** infringement risk
- **Suggests** design-around strategies

**Risk scoring:**
- **HIGH**: Direct overlap with valid claims
- **MEDIUM**: Overlap with dependent claims or ambiguous scope
- **LOW**: No material overlap or expired patents

### 4. Competitive IP Intelligence

Tracks competitor patent strategies:
- **Filing frequency** and trends
- **Technology focus areas**
- **Geographic filing patterns**
- **Patent family analysis**
- **Inventor networks**

**Insights:**
- "Company X filed 12 patents on bispecific antibodies in Q4 2024"
- "Filing surge in tumor-targeting ADCs (antibody-drug conjugates)"
- "Expanding protection to China + Japan (key markets)"

### 5. White Space Identification

Finds underprotected technology areas:
- **Sparse patent coverage**
- **Expiring key patents**
- **Overlooked combinations**
- **Geographic gaps**

**Example Output:**
```
Opportunity: IL-15 agonists for solid tumors
- Only 3 key patents (2 expiring 2026)
- No major pharma activity since 2020
- Strong preclinical rationale
- Estimated FTO risk: LOW
```

## Technical Architecture

### Patent Data Ingestion

**Data sources:**
- **Google Patents API** - 100M+ patents searchable
- **USPTO Bulk Data** - Full-text XML downloads
- **EPO OPS API** - European patent data
- **PatentsView** - USPTO analytics database

**Processing:**
- **PDF parsing** - Extract text from patent PDFs
- **XML parsing** - Structured data from USPTO XML
- **OCR** - Legacy patents (pre-2000)
- **Chemical structure extraction** - MOL files, SMILES

### NLP Pipeline

**Claim parsing:**
- Sentence segmentation
- Claim type classification (independent/dependent)
- Limitation extraction (entities + relationships)
- Claim tree construction

**Semantic search:**
- Patent embeddings using **SentenceBERT**
- Vector database (**Pinecone**) for similarity search
- Hybrid search (keyword + semantic)

### Knowledge Graph

**Neo4j graph database:**
- **Nodes**: Patents, Claims, Inventors, Assignees, Classifications
- **Relationships**: CITES, FILED_BY, COVERS, EXPIRES
- **Queries**: "Find all patents citing X that cover antibodies"

**Agent Workflow:**
1. Search 50,000+ ADC-related patents

## Technical Challenges Solved

### Challenge 1: Patent Language Complexity

**Problem:** Patents use dense legal + technical language

**Solution:**
- Fine-tuned language models on 100,000+ biotech patents
- Built domain-specific vocabulary (50,000+ terms)
- Trained on claim structure patterns
- Integrated USPTO classification system (CPC codes)

### Challenge 2: Chemical Structure Matching

**Problem:** Need to match chemical structures, not just text

**Solution:**
- Integrated RDKit for structure parsing
- Implemented Tanimoto similarity for structure search
- Used SMILES/InChI for standardization
- Built structure-claim mapping pipeline

### Challenge 3: Validity Assessment

**Problem:** Many patents are invalid or will be invalidated

**Solution:**
- Track patent prosecution history (office actions)
- Monitor PTAB proceedings (IPR, PGR challenges)
- Analyze prior art strength
- Flag weakness indicators (narrow claims, examiner rejections)

### Challenge 4: Scale

**Problem:** 10M+ patents, 100M+ claims to analyze

**Solution:**
- Distributed processing (Apache Spark)
- Incremental indexing (only new/updated patents)
- Smart caching (frequently accessed patents)
- Query optimization (targeted searches, not full scans)
