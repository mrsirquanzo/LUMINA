# Separating Hype from Reality in AI Drug Discovery

Every week brings another headline about how AI is going to transform drug discovery. But as someone who analyzes both the science and the business models, I think it's worth stepping back and asking: what's actually happening versus what's being promised?

## The Promise

The pitch is compelling: AI can predict molecular properties, optimize drug candidates, and accelerate preclinical development from years to months. Companies like Recursion, Insitro, and Absci have raised billions on this thesis.

And there's real science here. Machine learning models can:
- Predict binding affinity with reasonable accuracy
- Generate novel molecular structures
- Identify druggable targets from omics data
- Optimize ADMET properties earlier in development

## The Reality Check

But here's where we need nuance:

**Clinical validation is still pending.** Most AI-designed molecules haven't reached Phase 2/3 yet. We won't know if they're truly better until 2025-2027 when we see comparative clinical data.

**The timelines are still long.** Even with AI, you're not getting a drug to market in 2-3 years. The bottleneck has always been clinical development, not hit discovery. AI might compress 3-5 years of preclinical work into 12-18 months—meaningful, but not revolutionary.

**Success rates matter more than speed.** If AI-designed molecules have 30% higher Phase 2 success rates, that's far more valuable than shaving 12 months off preclinical timelines.

## What I'm Watching

The companies that will win are those that:
1. Focus on target selection, not just molecule design
2. Build integrated platforms (wet lab + dry lab)
3. Have clear clinical validation timelines
4. Are honest about what AI can and can't do

The hype cycle is real, but so is the potential. Let's just be clear-eyed about the timelines and what success actually looks like.
