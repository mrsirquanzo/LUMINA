# TCR vs CAR-T: Understanding the Mechanisms

Both TCR-based and CAR-T therapies are engineered T-cell approaches, but they work in fundamentally different ways. If you're evaluating cell therapy companies, understanding these differences is critical for assessing pipeline potential and competitive positioning.

## The Basic Difference

**CAR-T (Chimeric Antigen Receptor T-cell):**
- Recognizes antigens on the cell surface
- Doesn't need MHC/HLA presentation
- Works only for surface targets (CD19, BCMA, etc.)

**TCR (T-cell Receptor):**
- Recognizes peptides presented via MHC/HLA
- Can target intracellular proteins
- Expands the druggable target universe significantly

## Why This Matters for Investors

### Target Addressability
CAR-T is limited to ~20-30 well-characterized surface antigens. TCR can theoretically target thousands of intracellular proteins—including tumor-specific neoantigens, cancer-testis antigens, and viral antigens.

This is why companies like Immunocore (ImmTAC platform) and Lyell are betting on TCR: the target space is far larger.

### Solid Tumor Challenge
CAR-T has struggled in solid tumors due to:
- Antigen heterogeneity
- Tumor microenvironment suppression
- On-target, off-tumor toxicity

TCR approaches may have advantages here because they can target tumor-specific neoantigens with lower off-tumor expression. But they face their own challenges with MHC restriction and T-cell trafficking.

### Manufacturing Complexity
Both require autologous or allogeneic manufacturing, but TCR engineering has additional complexity around:
- Affinity tuning (too high = toxicity)
- Pairing optimization (to avoid mispairing)
- HLA restriction (patient selection)

## What to Watch

Key clinical questions for TCR platforms:
1. **Efficacy in solid tumors** – Can they demonstrate durable responses where CAR-T has failed?
2. **Safety profile** – On-target, off-tumor toxicity remains a risk
3. **Manufacturing scale** – Can they produce cells cost-effectively?

The next 2-3 years will be critical as we see Phase 2 data from multiple TCR programs. Companies with validated target selection strategies and manufacturing advantages will separate from the pack.
