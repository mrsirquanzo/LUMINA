# Why Biotech M&A is Accelerating in 2025

We're seeing a clear uptick in biotech M&A activity in early 2025. After a quiet 2023-2024, the deals are coming back. Here's what's driving it and what to expect.

## The Numbers

- **Q4 2024:** $47B in announced deals (up 85% YoY)
- **Average premium:** 60-80% over 30-day VWAP
- **Target profile:** Clinical-stage assets, particularly in oncology and rare disease

Big pharma has been relatively quiet on M&A for 18 months. Now they're back.

## Three Forces Driving Consolidation

### 1. Patent Cliffs Are Real
Major revenue losses coming 2025-2028 for blockbusters:
- Humira biosimilars already eroding AbbVie revenue
- Keytruda loses exclusivity 2028 (Merck's $25B problem)
- Eliquis, Imbruvica, Revlimid—all facing patent expiration

Pharma needs pipeline replenishment. Organic R&D hasn't delivered enough late-stage assets.

### 2. Biotech Valuations Reset
After the 2021 peak, many biotechs are trading at 2-3x cash despite clinical assets. This creates attractive entry points for acquirers with longer time horizons.

Companies with Phase 2 data that were valued at $2-3B in 2021 are now $400-600M. If you're big pharma and you believe in the asset, that's a compelling risk/reward.

### 3. Capital Markets Still Tight
IPO window remains mostly closed. Crossover investors pulled back. Series B+ funding is constrained.

This creates pressure on biotechs to partner or exit earlier than they would have in 2020-2021. Strategic acquirers can step in where the public markets won't.

## What I'm Watching

**Areas of focus:**
- Oncology (always)
- Obesity/metabolic disease (post-GLP-1 craze)
- Rare disease (high margin, clear regulatory path)
- Cell & gene therapy platforms (for platform acqui-hires)

**Likely acquirers:**
- Merck (Keytruda cliff pressure)
- Bristol Myers (Revlimid/Opdivo challenges)
- AbbVie (rebuilding post-Allergan)
- Gilead (flush with cash, needs pipeline)

## The Risk

If markets improve and biotech valuations re-rate upward, we could see deal flow slow as targets become too expensive. But for now, the conditions favor continued M&A activity through 2025.
